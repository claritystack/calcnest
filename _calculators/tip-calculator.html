How to Calculate a Tip in Your Head

To find 10%, move the decimal one place left. On an $85 bill, 10% = $8.50. For 20%, double it: $17.00. For 15%, take 10% and add half: $8.50 + $4.25 = $12.75.

Example — $64.00 bill
10% → $6.40
15% → $6.40 + $3.20 = $9.60
20% → $6.40 × 2 = $12.80
25% → $6.40 × 2.5 = $16.00

Should You Tip Before or After Tax?

Most Americans tip on the pre-tax subtotal. The difference is small on a typical bill, but in high-tax states like New York or California it can add up on larger bills. Either approach is widely accepted.

What Is a Good Tip at a Restaurant?

The US standard is 15%–20% for sit-down service. 20% has become the new normal in many cities. For counter service or fast casual, tipping is optional — $1–$2 is fine if you choose to. Restaurants often automatically add 18%–20% for groups of 6 or more, so always check the bill first.

Why Do Americans Tip?

In the US, tipped workers like restaurant servers often earn a lower base wage — sometimes as low as $2.13/hr federally — with tips making up the majority of their actual income. This system differs from most of the world, which is why tipping culture in America is stronger and more expected than almost anywhere else.
