How to Calculate a Tip in Your Head

No phone? No problem. Here's a quick mental math trick used by most Americans:

To find 10%, just move the decimal one place to the left. On a $85 bill, 10% = $8.50. For 20%, double that: $17.00. For 15%, take 10% and add half: $8.50 + $4.25 = $12.75.

Example — $64.00 bill
10% → $6.40
15% → $6.40 + $3.20 = $9.60
20% → $6.40 × 2 = $12.80
25% → $6.40 × 2 + $6.40 / 2 = $16.00

Should You Tip Before or After Tax?

Most Americans tip on the pre-tax subtotal. The difference is small on a typical bill, but in high-tax states like New York or California it can add up. Either approach is acceptable — what matters more is the percentage you choose.

What Is a Good Tip at a Restaurant?

The US standard is 15%–20% for sit-down service. 20% has become the new normal in many cities. For counter service or fast casual, tipping is optional — $1–$2 is fine if you choose to. Some restaurants automatically add an 18%–20% gratuity for groups of 6 or more, so always check the bill first.

Why Do Americans Tip?

In the US, tipped workers like restaurant servers often earn a lower base wage, with tips making up the majority of their actual income. This system is different from most of the world — which is why tipping culture in America is stronger and more expected than almost anywhere else.
